## 🚀 181. Employees Earning More Than Their Managers

### 💡 Idea

Both employees and managers are stored in the same `Employee` table. To compare an employee's salary with their manager's salary, we can perform a **self join** by joining the table with itself.

An `INNER JOIN` is used because only employees who have a manager are relevant for this comparison.

---

### 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT e.name AS Employee
FROM Employee e
INNER JOIN Employee m
ON e.managerId = m.id
WHERE e.salary > m.salary;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Perform a **self join** on the `Employee` table.
2. Treat `e` as the employee and `m` as the manager.
3. Match each employee with their manager using `e.managerId = m.id`.
4. Compare the employee's salary with the manager's salary.
5. Return the names of employees whose salary is greater than their manager's salary.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(depends on the join operation and indexing on `id`)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(1)` *(excluding the output result set)*
