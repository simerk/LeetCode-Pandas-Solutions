## 🚀 183. Customers Who Never Order

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to find customers who have **never placed an order**.

First, retrieve the IDs of all customers who have placed at least one order. Then, use a `NOT IN` subquery to exclude those customers from the `Customers` table.

---

### 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT name AS Customers
FROM Customers
WHERE id NOT IN (
    SELECT DISTINCT customerId
    FROM Orders );
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. The subquery retrieves the `customerId` of every customer who has placed an order.
2. `DISTINCT` ensures that each customer ID appears only once.
3. The outer query selects all customers from the `Customers` table.
4. `NOT IN` excludes customers whose IDs are returned by the subquery.
5. The remaining customers are those who have never placed an order.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(depends on the number of rows and indexing)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(stores the list of customer IDs returned by the subquery)*
