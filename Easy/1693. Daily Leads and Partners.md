## 🚀 1693. Daily Leads and Partners

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to calculate, for each date and make:

- The number of **unique leads**.
- The number of **unique partners**.

Since the same lead or partner may appear multiple times, we use `COUNT(DISTINCT ...)` to count only unique values.

---

## 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT date_id, make_name,
COUNT(DISTINCT lead_id) AS unique_leads,
COUNT(DISTINCT partner_id) AS unique_partners
FROM DailySales
GROUP BY date_id, make_name;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Group the records by `date_id` and `make_name`.
2. Use `COUNT(DISTINCT lead_id)` to count the unique leads for each group.
3. Use `COUNT(DISTINCT partner_id)` to count the unique partners for each group.
4. Return the results for every date and make combination.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(single scan followed by grouping)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(stores grouped results)*
