## 🚀 1327. List the Products Ordered in a Period

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to find the products that were ordered **at least 100 units** during **February 2020**.

We first filter the orders placed in February 2020, then group the records by product and calculate the total number of units ordered. Finally, we return only those products whose total units are at least **100**.

---

## 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT p.product_name, SUM(o.unit) AS unit
FROM Products p
INNER JOIN Orders o
ON p.product_id = o.product_id
WHERE o.order_date BETWEEN '2020-02-01' AND '2020-02-29'
GROUP BY p.product_name
HAVING SUM(o.unit) >= 100;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Join the `Products` and `Orders` tables using `product_id`.
2. Filter the orders placed between **2020-02-01** and **2020-02-29**.
3. Group the records by product.
4. Calculate the total units ordered for each product using `SUM(unit)`.
5. Keep only the products whose total ordered units are at least **100**.
6. Return the product name and the total units ordered.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(single join followed by grouping)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(stores grouped products)*
