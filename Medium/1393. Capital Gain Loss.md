## 🚀 1393. Capital Gain/Loss

### 💡 Idea

The goal is to calculate the **capital gain or loss** for each stock.

- A **Buy** transaction represents money spent, so its price should be treated as **negative**.
- A **Sell** transaction represents money received, so its price should be treated as **positive**.

By assigning positive and negative signs to the prices and summing them for each stock, we obtain the total capital gain or loss.

---

## 💻 Solution

```sql
SELECT stock_name,
    SUM(
        CASE WHEN operation = 'Buy' THEN -price
        ELSE price
        END
       ) AS capital_gain_loss
FROM Stocks
GROUP BY stock_name;
```

---

### 🛠️ How It Works

1. Group the records by `stock_name`.
2. Use a `CASE` expression to:
   - Convert **Buy** prices into negative values.
   - Keep **Sell** prices as positive values.
3. Sum all adjusted prices for each stock.
4. The resulting sum represents the stock's capital gain or loss.

---

### ⏱️ Complexity

**Time Complexity:** `O(n)` *(single scan with grouping)*

**Space Complexity:** `O(n)` *(stores grouped stock names)*
